Security Council

Issues:

The Issue of the Sudanese Coup

The Issue of Kazakhstan


A message from your presidents:
Welcome to the AltyMUN 2022 Security Council! This council is for advanced delegates who are passionate and knowledgable about issues that threaten global security. We will be debating two urgent and pressing issues. The issue of Kazakhstan, especially the large rallies against fuel prices which turned into violent protests against the autocratic regime with rioters burning buildings and briefly seizing an airport. The president Kassym-Zhomart Tokayev, dismissed his government and accused foreign powers of stirring political unrest and has asked for military help from Russia. The second issue will be The issue of Sudan which has thousands who protested against being ruled by generals. This is after Abdalla Hamdok, Sudan's prime minister resigning which ends the power-sharing agreement between his civilian supporters and the army that was supposed to usher the country towards democracy.

In the Security Council, debate procedure is much the same as in other committees, however. you can use words like, 'demands' and 'commands' which you can't in other committees and the P5 have veto powers so they can strike down clauses and host private meetings, however if anything doesn't make sense don't worry we will explain the slightly different rules on the day!

Security Council have the power to protect lives from mortal danger and call forth global change that is why on the day we hope to create and pass legislation to witness this change and of course inspire a fruitful debate at all times. Looking forward to meeting you all on the day your Presidents of Security Council Katie Young and Lauren Fletcher.

Make sure to send your resolutions to 16KYoung@aggs.bfet.uk and 16LFletcher@aggs.bfet.uk by 23:59 on March 4th
